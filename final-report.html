Final Report

Final Report for Web Fundamentals (WDD130). Covers all four final project websites.

Client

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I decided to do an egg website because why would I make a website about anything else? I decided on fonts using Google Fonts, selected colors using coolors, and styled it to fit a playful and fun theme. I created a list of sections I wanted to include and found images and text placeholders for them. To create the text content, I had an AI agent inject text content into the placeholders before reviewing it and making edits.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I communicated with my developer in person only. My plan was very well developed and included all the details he needed, so there was little need for communication. He checked in with me occasionally to show me his progress, but it always looked great and I had no suggestions for him.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Absolutely! The site looked fantastic and matched my vision perfectly. I was impressed with how well he executed the plan I had given him. He did a great job of checking in when necessary without being annoying as well.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

Honestly, I don't think I would do anything differently. The thing I did that benefited the project the most was creating a very detailed plan for my developer to follow. I think that was one of the keys to the success of the project, so I would definitely do that again with future projects so I can ensure the developer has a good idea of my vision.

Lead Dev

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior devs?

I created a text groupchat with my junior developers and my client in it to communicate with everyone at once. This idea was good in concept because I could easily share updates, ask questions, and assign tasks in one place with maximum transparency. However, nobody in my group was ever really in class so it didn't matter.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

I attempted to communicate with him via text, but he never responded, so I had to talk to him in person in class. The big thing I had to ask him was to send me the content for the site (both images and text), which he kept saying he would send me but never did. I ended up just using picsum and AI generated text to fill in the content, but it would have been nice to have the actual content from him.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

I just published the site to GitHub Pages and shared the link with him. I also looked at it in person with him in class to make sure he was happy with it.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

I have already had experience with GitHub and GitHub Pages, so I didn't have any issues with it. It was pretty straightforward to publish the site and make updates as needed. We never had any merge conflicts since my junior devs didn't do anything.

Junior Dev

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?

I talked to my team leads in person in class. I collected information about what I was assigned to do and then completed it on my own. The biggest issue in communication was that one of my lead devs did the entire project himself and didn't assign me to a page. He never told me anything, I just went in one day and it was all done. I asked him if there was anything I could do to help and he said no, so I just left it at that.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

Yes, I knew what was expected of me (for the site I actually did work on). I was assigned to do the second subpage for the site, and I based my work off of both the home page and the site plan.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

I was not able to contribute to the first site because of the reason described above, but I was able to contribute to the second site. I created the html structure, copying the header and footer from the home page. Most of the styling already applied correctly from the home page styling, but I adapted it to fit my page.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?

No issues were encountered. I usually made my changes by pulling the code, changing it quickly, and then pushing it. I let my team know when I had made changes so they could pull before making their own changes, thereby avoiding merge conflicts.

Reflection

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The biggest challenge by far was the lack of participation from my junior developers and client. Neither of my two junior devs contributed anything to the project whatsoever and were in class a total of maybe 4 times between the two of them. My client also never sent me any of the content for the site, so I had to make it all up from scratch. I was able to overcome these challenges by just doing everything myself and ignoring the two subpages entirely. This led to a less ideal finished product, but it was what I was able to accomplish given the circumstances.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

It was fun to create a website plan, send it off, and see it come to life. I enjoyed the creative process of designing the site and then seeing my developers execute upon that vision.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would get a better team who actually came to class and did their work. At the very least it would have been more helpful if they told me they weren't going to do anything instead of just not showing up or communicating at all. It would have also been nice if the client had sent me the content for the site.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

If anything, I feel less confident in working on real-world projects. This experience taught me that a lot of people are unreliable and hard to work with. Neither of my teammates contributed anything to the project whatsoever, one of my senior devs didn't reach out to me even once and did everything himself, and my client didn't provide the resources I needed. This has taught me that projects can fall flat instantly if you don't have the right team. The one saving grace in the real-world is that if your teammates never show up, they get fired.
